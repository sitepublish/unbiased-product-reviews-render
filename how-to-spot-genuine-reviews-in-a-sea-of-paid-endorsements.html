Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common signs of a fake product review?
Warning signs include exclusively positive language, generic descriptions lacking specific details, review date clustering around product launch, reviewer accounts with limited history, and excessive use of the full product name as though following a marketing script.
What is the importance of testing products in real-world conditions?
Laboratory testing establishes theoretical performance limits while real-world testing reveals practical performance under typical conditions. The gap between these measurements indicates how much consumers should discount manufacturer specifications when making purchasing decisions.
How reliable are star rating systems for product evaluation?
Star rating systems compress complex multi-factor evaluation into a single number, losing critical nuance. A 4-star product might excel at performance but fail at durability. Reading full review text and examining category-specific scores provides more actionable purchasing information.
Why do some reviews contradict each other?
Different reviewers have different use cases, expectations, and testing conditions. A product that works perfectly for one person's needs may fall short for another. Reading multiple reviews helps you identify patterns and form a balanced opinion.
